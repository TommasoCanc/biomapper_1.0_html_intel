Checklist of the Italian fauna
...Today

The updated version of the Checklist of the Italian fauna was proposed in 2017 during the 7th Conference of the Italian Society for Evolutionary Biology by a group of members of the Scientific Committee for the Italian Fauna (CSFI). The new Checklist of the Italian fauna aims to update and digitalize the actual knowledge about the Italian animal biodiversity. The updated faunistic inventories will be free and available for specialists, students, amateurs, and public administrators, with the intent to be an essential tool to build a solid taxonomic backbone to produce reproducible datasets for any biological investigation to improve the efficiency of the conservation plans in Italy.

Checklist of the Italian fauna
Yesterday...

In 1991, inspired by the success of "Fauna d'Italia" and encouraged by the enthusiasm of Professors Alessandro Minelli and Sandro Ruffo and with the financial support of the Ministry for Environment, a group of researchers of CSFI launched the project Checklist of the Italian fauna. This initiative focused on producing an exhaustive faunistic inventory of species occurring in the whole Italian country. As a result, the first version Checklist of the Italian fauna led to collect information about more than 55,000 animal species, thanks to 272 specialists worldwide.

Fauna d'Italia
The origin

The editorial project "Fauna d'Italia" was promoted for the first time in 1952 by the CSFI. This initiative aimed to produce monographic volumes dedicated to collecting as much information as possible about the taxonomy and the biology of Italian Metazoa inhabiting marine, freshwater, and terrestrial ecosystems. To date, thanks to the effort of numerous zoologists, have been published 53 monograph volumes whom 12 dedicated to vertebrates and 31 about insects.
